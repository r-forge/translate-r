R Translation Teams

R version 2.1.0 and later support translations of program messages into different languages. A number of translation projects are already underway or completed. To offer your help with one of these, contact a team on the list below. To list your own translation project here, contact Duncan Murdoch <murdoch.duncan@gmail.com> or another member of R-core with the details.

The following pages include the information about internationalization of R:

  • Internationalization chapter of R extensions,
  • Internationalization in the R sources chapter of R Internals,
  • Internationalization and Localization chapter of R Installation and Administration.

R Manuals

The R Documentation files are licensed under the General Public License, version 2 or 3. This means that the pilot project to translate them into other languages has permission to reproduce them and translate them. It would be another aspect of the internationalization of R. Some results have been worked out can be check at [here]. By working with GNU gettext utilities, this project attempts to keep translation up-to-dated along with the development of R documentation. The project named with translate.R is housed in R-Forge. The portable object templates for R manuals are updated everyday or whenever changes are necessary. Please contact Chel Hee Lee<chl948@mail.usask.ca> for questions, comments, and/or voluntary participation.

Bug Report

Corrections to and comments on message translations should be sent to the maintainer of your language team as listed at http://translation.r-project.org. If you would like to use the R bug tracking system, please choose the translation item on the component field.
